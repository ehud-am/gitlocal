# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

GitLocal is built for people who want a calm, useful way to work with local repositories. We want the project community to reflect that same standard: respectful, practical, and welcoming to contributors with different backgrounds and levels of experience.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive in issues, pull requests, reviews, and discussions.
- Assume good intent, but accept correction when your words or actions have a negative effect.
- Keep feedback focused on the work, the user experience, and the technical tradeoffs.
- Use clear, inclusive language.
- Respect maintainers' time by keeping reports and proposals actionable.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, or personal attacks.
- Discriminatory language or behavior.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or attention.
- Trolling, sustained disruption, or bad-faith arguments.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Repeatedly ignoring maintainer direction in project spaces.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code of conduct, report it privately to the project maintainers through the repository owner's GitHub profile at <https://github.com/ehud-am> or another maintainer-provided contact channel. If no private channel is available, open a GitHub issue that asks for a maintainer contact path and does not include sensitive details.

Reports should include:

- what happened;
- where it happened;
- who was involved;
- any links, screenshots, or context that help maintainers understand the situation.

Maintainers will review reports as promptly as practical and will take action they consider appropriate for the safety and health of the project.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any appropriate action, including:

- clarifying expectations privately or publicly;
- editing or deleting comments;
- closing or locking issues or pull requests;
- temporarily or permanently limiting participation in the project.

Enforcement decisions are based on impact, context, and the need to keep the project healthy for contributors and users.
